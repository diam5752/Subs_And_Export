- [93ΡΦ4690ΩΖ-ΛΙ6](https://diavgeia.gov.gr/doc/93ΡΦ4690ΩΖ-ΛΙ6)

### Manual chain correction

The five ADAs are not five contracts. Committee-formation and approval documents were collapsed conservatively into three economic chains:

1. Contract / negotiation 8/2026, initially covering approximately two months.
2. Invitation / contract 12/2026, covering one additional month.
3. Invitation / contract 15/2026, covering one additional month.

### Officially supported facts

- Same authority and same service: building and grounds cleaning, CPV 90911200-8.
- Same supplier: IPIROTIKI FACILITY SERVICES S.A.
- Repeated use of negotiation without prior publication / exceptional procedure and explicit urgency.
- The first short bridge has a stated amount of €145,508.56 net, €180,430.61 including VAT.
- The following one-month bridge has a stated amount of €75,683.23 net, €93,847.21 including VAT.
- The next one-month bridge has a stated amount of €75,680.00 net, €93,843.20 including VAT.
- Conservative known gross exposure of the three bridge periods: **€368,121.02**.
- The documents describe an open procurement process affected by review / appeal timing.

### Strongest ordinary explanation

Cleaning in an oncology hospital cannot be interrupted, and the open procedure was delayed by procurement-review proceedings. This strongly explains the need for temporary continuity.

### Why it remains high priority

Even after accepting that explanation, the official record shows successive two-month and one-month exceptional bridges to the same supplier, for the same predictable essential service and material cumulative value. The appropriate audit question is whether the regular competition was planned and managed early enough and whether each successive exceptional period was strictly necessary and proportionate.
